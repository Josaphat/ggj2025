Things to Do

  • Gregor Westman Park: A can't-miss for any visitor to Rochester, see the beautiful Jenny River as she flows through the park toward Lake Ontario. Get your map of the park here.
  • Weak Museum of Torture Devices: Are you interested in Brazen Bulls? Iron Maidens? That stretchy thing in the Princess Bride? Well, this is the place for you. See a number of torture devices that are claimed to be medieval but probably just invented by the Victorians! Get your tickets here. Family discounts apply!
  • Private Market: The most secretive place of commerce in Rawchester! Bring your robes and Masonic emblem and maybe, just maybe, you'll get into this place under city hall. Don't know where to go? Just follow the pyramid eye iconography, you won't miss it.
    And there will be no orgies, we definitely don't have orgies here.

History

  • Abandoned Terrence Building: One of Rawchester's tallest buildings, the former home of the Rochester Psychiatric Hospital. The Terrence Building was first opened in 1959 as a psychiatric hospital building in addition to housing a geriatrics facility.[4] It became the new home of the Rochester State Hospital, which had existed since 1891 after replacing the Monroe County Insane Asylum.[5][6] The tower had a different department on each floor, in addition to a general hospital on the 13th and 14th floor, with the 5th floor housing the criminally insane and the basement holding a lab and morgue. During the building's history, allegations of patient abuse within the tower's premises often surfaced.[7] Furthermore, many of the patients were treated as "lost causes, impossible to reintegrate with society" and were essentially locked away inside the tower.[8]

    The tower was eventually closed down in 1995. See an archived version of their website here.
